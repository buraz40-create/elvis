Custom Chair Upholstery — From Bare Frame to Finished Piece

Published December 27, 2012  ·  Elvis Upholstery & Draperies  ·  ← Back to Blog

One of the most satisfying projects in any upholsterer's repertoire is the complete chair reupholstery — taking a piece that has lived a long life, stripping it to the bare frame, rebuilding it properly from the foundation up, and delivering it back better than it was the day it was first made. This post documents exactly that process, from the initial frame assessment all the way through to the finished, client-ready piece — every step completed in our Atlantic Beach workshop at 1079 Atlantic Blvd #7.

Step 1: Assessing the Frame

Before a single piece of fabric is touched, the chair is stripped completely. Old fabric, foam, batting, webbing, and tacking strips are removed until only the bare frame remains. This is where the real assessment happens. We inspect every joint, every dowel, every corner block and rail for signs of looseness, damage, or deterioration. A beautiful fabric on a compromised frame is a wasted investment — we fix the foundation first, every time.

For this commission, the hardwood frame was structurally sound but had three loose corner blocks that needed re-gluing and clamping overnight. The sinuous springs were in excellent condition and required only minor re-tying. Once repairs were complete and fully cured, we were ready to rebuild.

Step 2: Foundation — Webbing, Springs and Foam

New webbing was applied to the seat deck, stretched to the correct tension and interlaced in both directions before being stapled securely at each rail. Springs were re-tied and inspected. High-density polyfoam was then cut by hand to the precise shape of the seat, inside back, and arm pads — with the density specification chosen to match the chair's intended use. The foam was wrapped in Dacron batting to create that soft, rounded profile that holds its shape over years of use.

Step 3: Fabric Application

The fabric for this project — a cream performance-grade textured weave chosen from our 300+ swatch collection in our Atlantic Beach showroom — was laid out with pattern repeats centred across each key face of the chair. Inside elements were upholstered first: inside back, inside arms, seat deck. Each section was pulled to consistent tension, stapled along every rail, and trimmed cleanly. Welt cord seams were sewn, pressed, and hand-tacked at every visible joint line.

The outside back — the most visible face — was applied last and finished to the same standard as the inside. Decorative legs were fitted, the dust cover hand-tacked to the underside, and the piece was given a final quality inspection before being approved for delivery.

The Finished Result

From intake to delivery, this commission took four business days. The client received a chair that was structurally sounder, more comfortable, and more beautiful than the day it was first purchased — in a fabric that is genuinely personal to their home. That is the Elvis Upholstery standard, and it applies to every single piece we produce.
