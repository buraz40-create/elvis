Why Reupholster Instead of Replace?

The furniture industry will always try to sell you something new. But the honest truth — and we've built 27 years of business on honesty — is that most quality furniture is worth far more than its resale price suggests. The solid hardwood frames, the coil spring systems, the careful joinery of older pieces simply cannot be replicated at today's mass-market price points.

When you bring a piece to Elvis Upholstery, our first step is always a thorough frame inspection. If the bones are good — and they often are — reupholstery delivers a result that's better, more personal, and more durable than anything you'd find in a furniture store at twice the cost. We've reupholstered pieces that have since lasted another 20 years and become the centerpieces of their rooms.

Reupholstery is also the environmentally responsible choice. Every piece we restore is a piece that stays out of a landfill — and a piece of craftsmanship that continues to have a life.

Our Furniture Reupholstery Process

Step 1: Free Consultation & Frame Assessment

Every project begins with a conversation. Call us at (904) 242-8778 or stop by our showroom at 1079 Atlantic Blvd #7 in Atlantic Beach. We'll examine your piece in person, assess the frame condition, identify any structural repairs needed, and give you an honest written estimate — typically within 24 hours. There's no pressure, no sales pitch. Just expertise.

Step 2: Fabric Selection

This is often the most enjoyable part of the process. Our Atlantic Beach showroom carries over 300 fabric swatches covering every aesthetic and performance specification you can imagine. From Sunbrella solution-dyed acrylics for coastal and outdoor applications, to Crypton stain-proof performance fabrics for family rooms, to Belgian linen, cut velvet, and patterned jacquard for more formal spaces — the selection is extraordinary.

We also welcome customer's own material (COM). If you've found the perfect fabric from a designer, an online source, or our affiliated fabric store at , we'll work with it. Our team will calculate the yardage requirements and provide guidance on pattern matching, directional layouts, and any special considerations.

Step 3: Structural Repair & Preparation

Before a single yard of fabric is cut, we prepare your piece properly. This means stripping the old upholstery completely, inspecting every joint and dowel, tightening or replacing the webbing, and evaluating the spring system. If sinuous springs need replacing, coil springs need re-tying, or a frame needs re-gluing and clamping, we handle all of it — because a beautiful fabric on a weak frame is a waste of everyone's investment.

We then apply the appropriate foam specification for your piece. Seat cushions, back cushions, and decorative elements all have different density and ILD requirements. We cut every piece of foam by hand and wrap it in Dacron batting to create that signature smooth, rounded profile that holds its shape over time.

Step 4: Expert Handcrafted Upholstery

This is where 27 years of skill shows. Our master upholsterers apply fabric with the same precision a tailor brings to a bespoke suit. Every seam is planned before cutting, every pattern is matched, every welt cord is sewn and applied by hand. Tufting buttons are set at exact intervals. Nailhead trim is placed piece by piece. Skirts are mitered at the corners. Nothing is rushed, nothing is guessed.

The result is a piece that not only looks exceptional — it feels exceptional. The kind of furniture that makes you stop every time you walk by it.

Step 5: White-Glove Delivery & Installation

When your piece is complete, we contact you for pickup or arrange delivery. For larger commissions and commercial projects, we offer white-glove delivery and placement across Jacksonville, Atlantic Beach, Jacksonville Beach, Neptune Beach, Ponte Vedra Beach, St. Augustine, and Amelia Island. Your piece arrives wrapped, protected, and placed exactly where you want it.

Frequently Asked Questions: Furniture Reupholstery in Jacksonville

How much does furniture reupholstery cost in Jacksonville, FL?
The cost of furniture reupholstery in Jacksonville, FL varies depending on the size of the piece, the complexity of the design, and your fabric selection. A standard accent chair typically ranges from $400–$900, while a full sofa can range from $1,200–$3,500 or more. All estimates from Elvis Upholstery are written, itemized, and provided free of charge. Contact us at (904) 242-8778 or stop by our Atlantic Beach showroom at 1079 Atlantic Blvd #7.
Is it worth reupholstering furniture instead of buying new?
In most cases, yes — especially for pieces with solid hardwood frames, heirloom value, or quality construction. Custom upholstery also gives you control over every detail: fabric, color, texture, cushion density, and trim. The result is a piece that's genuinely one-of-a-kind and often more durable than comparable new furniture at a higher price point.
How long does furniture reupholstery take?
Most residential reupholstery projects at Elvis Upholstery are completed within 2–4 weeks from the time fabric is confirmed and the piece is received. Rush timelines can sometimes be accommodated for commercial clients. We provide a clear, realistic timeline at the time of your free estimate — and we stick to it.
What types of furniture can you reupholster?
We reupholster virtually any upholstered piece: sofas, sectionals, armchairs, wingback chairs, dining chairs, ottomans, chaises, headboards, benches, window seats, and built-in banquettes. We also handle antique and heirloom pieces requiring period-appropriate restoration, and commercial seating for restaurants, hotels, and offices.

Yes. We offer pickup and white-glove delivery for reupholstery projects throughout Jacksonville, Atlantic Beach, Jacksonville Beach, Neptune Beach, Ponte Vedra Beach, St. Augustine, and Amelia Island. Contact us at (904) 242-8778 to discuss your project and schedule a convenient pickup time.
