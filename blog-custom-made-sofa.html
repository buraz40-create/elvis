Custom Made Sofa — Built From Scratch in Our Atlantic Beach Workshop

Published December 27, 2012  ·  Elvis Upholstery & Draperies  ·  ← Back to Blog

Building a sofa from scratch is the fullest expression of what a custom upholstery studio can offer. The client arrives with a vision — or simply a space that off-the-shelf furniture cannot serve — and every dimension, material, spring system, and fabric choice is made from scratch. No catalogue compromises. This post documents one such commission: a three-seat custom sofa built entirely in our Atlantic Beach workshop, from the first saw cut to the final welt cord seam.

The Brief: Dimensions That Don't Exist in Retail

The client had a long, low-ceilinged living room where standard sofa proportions simply didn't work. The seat needed to be slightly deeper than average, the back lower than most retail options, and the overall length tailored to fill a specific wall without overwhelming it. We discussed arm style (a streamlined track arm), leg finish (brushed brass), cushion configuration (three loose seat cushions, two loose back cushions), and fabric preferences before producing a full written specification.

Frame Construction: Kiln-Dried Hardwood

The frame was cut from kiln-dried hardwood — a non-negotiable material choice for any piece expected to last decades. Kiln-dried timber is dimensionally stable, resistant to warping, and provides the structural integrity that a well-built sofa requires. All joints were corner-blocked and glued, then clamped and allowed to cure fully before proceeding. The completed frame was stress-tested before any further work began.

Eight-Way Hand-Tied Springs and Suspension

We specified an eight-way hand-tied coil spring system for this commission — considered the gold standard of sofa suspension for over a century. Each spring is tied individually in eight directions to a webbing base, creating a suspension that moves as a unified surface and distributes weight with exceptional evenness. This is the foundation of genuine seating comfort, and it takes considerably longer to execute than modern alternatives — for good reason.

Foam, Dacron and Cushion Construction

Seat cushions were constructed with a high-density polyfoam core wrapped in a generous Dacron batting layer, creating the characteristic soft-but-supportive profile that retains its shape after years of use. Back cushions used a polyfoam and feather-down combination for deeper comfort. Every cushion cover was sewn with precisely matched seams and fitted to the cushion forms with consistent tension throughout.

Fabric Selection and Application

The fabric — a COM linen-blend in warm ivory sourced from our fabric supply partner at — was laid out with every cut planned to minimise waste and optimise directional alignment across all three seat cushion faces. Welt cord seams finish every visible edge. The result is a sofa that belongs in this home and nowhere else: built to the client's dimensions, in the client's fabric, to a standard that retail simply cannot match.
